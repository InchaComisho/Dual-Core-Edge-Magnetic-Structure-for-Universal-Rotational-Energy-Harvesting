# Conceptual Lineage: From Thermal Power Plant Retrofit to Universal Rotational Energy Harvesting

## Source NOTE series and design evolution

This repository is not an isolated generator idea. The **Dual-Core Edge Magnetic Structure for Universal Rotational Energy Harvesting** developed from a series of Japanese NOTE articles on thermal power plant retrofitting, inverse-phase coil recovery, motor energy recovery, vertical-axis turbine generation, and open-invention energy circulation.

The current GitHub version generalizes those earlier ideas into a cautious, technically reviewable open architecture for rotational energy harvesting.

---

## 1. Original problem: thermal power plant CO2 reduction

The early design context was not simply to build a new renewable generator. The origin was a retrofit proposal for existing thermal power plants.

The initial model attempted to reduce the net fossil-fuel burden of old thermal power infrastructure by adding secondary energy-recovery layers:

- spiral / whirlpool water flow,
- blade-driven rotation,
- rotating magnets,
- fixed coils,
- rectification,
- transformation / inversion,
- batteries,
- supplemental grid transmission.

In this origin model, existing thermal power infrastructure is not immediately discarded. Instead, wasted or secondary flows inside the plant are treated as recoverable rotational-energy sources.

This is the conceptual bridge from:

```text
thermal power retrofit
-> spiral / whirlpool flow recovery
-> rotational magnetic generation
-> battery buffering
-> supplemental grid output
-> universal rotational energy harvesting
```

## 2. Key source NOTE articles

### 2.1 Motor energy recovery through inverse-phase coils

**Title:** 概要：逆位相反転コイルでのモーター内エネルギー回収とは？  
**Published:** July 21, 2025  
**URL:** https://note.com/inchacomusho/n/n92aaed58fca4

This article frames inverse-phase coil recovery as a way to recover part of the rotational energy or back-EMF behavior associated with motor operation. It also compares the idea to known mechanisms such as bicycle hub dynamos, regenerative braking, EV energy recovery, and regenerative inverters.

Relevance to this repository:

- establishes the idea of recovering energy from existing rotation,
- introduces rectification / boost / stabilization requirements,
- clarifies that practical limits include cost, size, control interference, heat, and use-case constraints.

## 4. How the concept evolved

The conceptual evolution can be summarized as follows:

```text
1. Recover energy from motor rotation and back-EMF-like behavior
2. Add inverse-phase / generation-side coils
3. Rectify, boost, stabilize, and store recovered energy
4. Apply the idea to thermal power plant retrofit systems
5. Use spiral / whirlpool flow and vertical-axis rotation
6. Add Dual Core-Edge generation: center-axis + outer-ring magnetic recovery
7. Generalize the architecture to wind, water, tidal, wave, drainage, and artificial flow systems
```

The present repository corresponds to Step 7: generalization into a universal rotational energy harvesting architecture.

## 5. Technical caution

The original NOTE articles contain early-stage open-invention proposals. They should not be read as validated performance claims.

For GitHub technical documentation, the safe framing is:

- concept: valid as an open design hypothesis,
- physics basis: electromagnetic induction and rotational energy conversion,
- performance: unverified until measured,
- efficiency: must be calculated from mechanical input and electrical output,
- claims: must avoid perpetual motion, free energy, guaranteed efficiency, or input-free generation.

## 6. Why this lineage matters

The value of recording this lineage is that it shows the idea did not begin as an abstract generator concept.

It began as an attempt to solve a practical transitional problem:

> How can existing fossil-fuel and industrial infrastructure be modified so that wasted rotational, thermal, water-flow, and airflow energy can be recovered locally instead of discarded?
